# Lesson Content

## warm-up

### The Engine of the Sentence

Nouns are just things. Verbs are **actions**. They make things happen.
Up until now, we just pointed at things ("This is a book"). Now, we are going to *do* things with them ("I **read** the book").

In English, verbs are lazy. "I read, you read, we read, they read." Only "he reads" changes.
In Ukrainian, verbs are **alive**. They change their ending for every single person. It is like a secret handshake between the Subject and the Verb.

Today we meet **Class I**: The verbs that usually end in **-Ð°Ñ‚Ð¸**.

> ðŸ’¡ **Did You Know?**
>
> Because the verb ending tells you *who* is doing the action, you can often drop the pronoun!
> - **Ð¯ Ñ‡Ð¸Ñ‚Ð°ÑŽ.** (I read.)
> - **Ð§Ð¸Ñ‚Ð°ÑŽ.** (Reading-I.)
> It is faster and more natural.
