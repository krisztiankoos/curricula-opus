# Lesson Content

## warm-up

### The Invisible Verb

Here's one of the coolest things about Ukrainian: in the present tense, you don't need "to be"!

| English | Ukrainian |
|---------|-----------|
| I **am** a student | Ð¯ ÑÑ‚ÑƒÐ´ÐµÐ½Ñ‚ (Ya student) |

See? No "am," "is," or "are"! The verb just disappears. This is called the **zero copula**.

> ðŸ’¡ **Did You Know?**
>
> Many languages have the zero copula, including Russian, Arabic, and Hungarian. English used to have it too in some dialects! "You ready?" instead of "Are you ready?" is a trace of this.

### Why No "To Be"?

Ukrainian doesn't need "to be" in the present tense because:
- The pronoun tells you WHO
- The noun/adjective tells you WHAT
- Context does the rest!
