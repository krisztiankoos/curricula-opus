In Ukrainian, it is very easy to talk about the future! You just need the helper verb **Ğ±ÑƒÑ‚Ğ¸** (audio_to be) + **Infinitive**.

> Unlike English ("I will read"), Ukrainian uses one word for "will be" conjugated for the person, plus the unchanged verb dictionary form!
> - Ğ¯ **Ğ±ÑƒĞ´Ñƒ** ÑĞ¿Ğ°Ñ‚Ğ¸. (I will sleep).
> - ĞœĞ¸ **Ğ±ÑƒĞ´ĞµĞ¼Ğ¾** Ñ—ÑÑ‚Ğ¸. (We will eat).

## Cultural Insight: Planning

> [!cultural] "Maybe" Culture
> Ukrainians often say **"ĞŸĞ¾Ğ±Ğ°Ñ‡Ğ¸Ğ¼Ğ¾"** (We will see) when talking about plans. It is a polite way to say "maybe" or "I am not sure yet".
> Also, keep in mind: **"Ğ—Ñ–Ğ´Ğ·Ğ²Ğ¾Ğ½Ğ¸Ğ¼Ğ¾ÑÑ!"** (Let's call each other!) often means "Goodbye" rather than a concrete plan to call.
