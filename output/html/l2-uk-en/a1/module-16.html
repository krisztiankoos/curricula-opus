The Art of Knowing What is Missing

Presentation: The Genitive of Negation

When something exists, it is Nominative (Subject).
When something is absent, it becomes Genitive. The logic is: "There is a lack of [Object]".

Deep Dive: The logic of -–ê vs -–£ üß†

For Masculine nouns in Genitive, you have a choice: -–∞ or -—É?
