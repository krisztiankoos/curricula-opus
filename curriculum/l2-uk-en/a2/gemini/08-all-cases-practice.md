# Lesson Content

## warm-up

### The Matrix of Grammar

You have learned them all. Seven cases. Seven ways a word can change to show its role.
Now we step back and see the whole picture.

1.  **Nominative:** The Subject (Who is doing it?)
2.  **Genitive:** The Absence/Owner (Of/From/No)
3.  **Dative:** The Receiver (To/For)
4.  **Accusative:** The Target (Whom/What?)
5.  **Instrumental:** The Tool/Companion (With/By)
6.  **Locative:** The Location (In/On)
7.  **Vocative:** The Call (Hey!)

Think of a sentence as a stage play. The **Nominative** is the actor. The **Accusative** is the prop they handle. The **Instrumental** is the tool they use. The **Dative** is the audience member they give it to. The **Locative** is the stage itself.

Today is about **fluency**. We will mix them up and train your brain to pick the right one instantly.

### The Golden Questions

To choose the right case, you must ask the right question. Do not translate from English; analyze the **relationship**.

1.  **Is it doing the action?** -> Nominative.

2.  **Is it receiving something?** -> Dative.

   > [!answer] Tool/With
