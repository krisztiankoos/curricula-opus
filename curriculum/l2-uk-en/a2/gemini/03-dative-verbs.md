# Lesson Content

## warm-up

### The Dative Club

In Ukrainian, verbs are the bosses of the sentence. They tell the nouns what to do and which "costume" (case) to wear. Most verbs are simple: they want a direct object in the Accusative case (I see **mom**).

But there is an exclusive group of verbs that are picky. They refuse to work with the Accusative. They demand the **Dative**. We call them the **Dative Only Club**.

Why? Think about the logic. When you help someone, you are **giving** help TO them. When you trust someone, you are **giving** trust TO them. These verbs describe an impact or influence on a person, not a direct action done *to* an object.
