> üìú **Historical Note: The Origins of Ukrainian Prefixes**
> 
> The Ukrainian aspect system developed over centuries. Many prefixes that today create perfective aspect (–ø—Ä–æ-, –Ω–∞-, –∑-) once had concrete spatial meanings. > üîó **Language Link: English vs Ukrainian Aspect**
> 
> English doesn't have grammatical aspect the way Ukrainian does. In English, we use context and additional words: "I was reading" vs "I read it" vs "I finished reading." Ukrainian bakes all this meaning into the verb form itself: ¬´—á–∏—Ç–∞–≤¬ª vs ¬´–ø—Ä–æ—á–∏—Ç–∞–≤¬ª. This is both more efficient (fewer words needed) and more precise (no ambiguity about whether you finished).
